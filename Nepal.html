Temples & Festivals

Nepal celebrates vibrant festivals like Dashain, Tihar, Holi, and more in thousands of temples across the country. These festivals symbolize cultural richness, unity, and spiritual heritage passed through generations.

Ethnic Diversity

With over 120 ethnic groups and 123 languages, Nepal is a cultural mosaic. Ethnic communities like the Newars, Tharus, Gurungs, Magars, and Sherpas live in harmony, each contributing unique customs, cuisines, music, dance, and beliefs.

Himalayan Peaks

Nepal is home to 8 of the world's 10 tallest peaks, including Mt. Everest (Sagarmatha). These snow-capped mountains attract climbers, trekkers, and spiritual seekers from around the world.

Flora & Fauna

Nepal has remarkable biodiversity ranging from subtropical forests to alpine meadows. Wildlife includes endangered species like the one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tiger, red panda, and snow leopard.

UNESCO Sites

Nepal has 10 UNESCO World Heritage Sites including Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, and the ancient cities of Bhaktapur, Patan, and Kathmandu.

Adventure & Trekking

Nepal offers world-class trekking and adventure experiences such as the Everest Base Camp trek, Annapurna Circuit, paragliding in Pokhara, white-water rafting, and jungle safaris.
